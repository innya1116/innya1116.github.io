Cumulative number of green start-up partnerships: 100 by 2025

To promptly alleviate environmental problems, such as climate change and plastic pollution, both government-level and corporate-level endeavors play a pivotal role. SK innovation has been undertaking such roles actively by fostering green start-ups and supporting the green ecosystems since 2019. Transition to a green, low-carbon society can be accelerated when start-ups with various innovative solutions grow and expand their businesses.

Based on this belief, SK innovation has implemented the Environment and Social Business Contest and SV2 Impact Partnering in 2019, which eventually developed into a collaborative project with the Ministry of SMEs and Start-ups. "EGG" is the first Green Alliance in Korea which involved a collaboration between government agencies, conglomerates and external stakeholders such as impact funds to discover partnerships to foster green start-up companies.

To accomplish the key task of "enhancing green ecosystem and green start-up partnerships", we will establish multiple new alliance to discover and foster green start-ups globally and establish stable, long-term projects pertinent to the "EGG" program. This project is expected to deliver a wide range of environmental, social and economic values as well as accelerate a transformation of the current economic structure into a one driven by medium-sized and venture companies, contributing to quality jobs and more equitable business ecosystems.
